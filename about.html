Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)

What is CAD?

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) is a condition where the arteries supplying blood to the heart become narrowed or blocked due to plaque buildup, leading to reduced blood flow.

Causes

CAD is primarily caused by atherosclerosis, which is the buildup of fatty deposits in the coronary arteries. Risk factors include high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, high blood pressure, obesity, and a sedentary lifestyle.

Symptoms

Common symptoms include chest pain (angina), shortness of breath, fatigue, nausea, dizziness, and in severe cases, heart attack.

Diagnosis

Doctors diagnose CAD using tests like electrocardiograms (ECG), stress tests, coronary angiography, and CT scans to assess blood flow and detect blockages.

Prevention

Maintaining a healthy diet, regular exercise, quitting smoking, managing stress, and regular health check-ups can help reduce the risk of CAD.

Treatment

Treatment options include lifestyle changes, medications (such as statins, beta-blockers, and aspirin), and in some cases, medical procedures like angioplasty, stent placement, or bypass surgery.

Complications

If left untreated, CAD can lead to complications such as heart failure, arrhythmias, and sudden cardiac arrest, making early diagnosis and management essential.
